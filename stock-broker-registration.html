REGISTRATION STEPS

Stock Broker Registration Process

Three-step pathway to become a SEBI registered stock broker

1

Exchange Membership

Apply for membership on NSE/BSE/MSEI. Meet minimum capital requirements and infrastructure standards set by exchange

2

SEBI Registration

Submit Form B with required documents. SEBI verification of eligibility criteria, capital adequacy, and compliance framework

3

Registration Certificate

Obtain SEBI registration certificate. Begin trading operations while maintaining ongoing compliance obligations

KEY REQUIREMENTS

Compliance Framework

Essential compliance areas for stock brokers

Capital Adequacy

Maintain minimum net worth: ₹1 Cr (BSE/NSE Cash), ₹5 Cr (Futures & Options), ₹10 Cr (All segments). Regular capital monitoring and reporting required

KYC & Client Protection

Implement comprehensive KYC procedures, maintain client protection through separate accounts, manage investor grievances within specified timeframes

Segregation of Funds

Maintain separate bank accounts for client funds, separate securities custody, prevent commingling of broker and client assets

Risk Management

Implement risk management systems, maintain adequate margin collection, monitor open positions, manage settlement risks
