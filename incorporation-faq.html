Incorporation Rules & Their Importance

The Companies Act, 2013 sets the broad legal framework. But it doesn't specify every procedural detail. To fill this gap, the Central Government notified the Companies (Incorporation) Rules, 2014 (effective 1 April 2014, last amended 2024).

These Rules tell you exactly:

  • Which form to file
  • What documents to attach
  • What timelines to follow
  • What fees to pay — for every step from name reservation to Certificate of Incorporation

The Rules are amended frequently. Major amendments: 2015, 2016 (SPICe introduced), 2018, 2019, 2020 (SPICe+ launched), 2021 (OPC reforms), 2023 (INC-4 simplification), 2024.

Always refer to the latest version on: mca.gov.in → Acts & Rules → E-Books → Rules

Types of Companies & OPC Rules

Under Rule 3, only a natural person who is an Indian citizen can form an OPC. Key 2021 update: The earlier requirement of residing in India for 182 days was changed to 120 days AND the residency condition itself was made optional — meaning NRIs and Indian citizens abroad can now form an OPC.

  • Must be a natural person (not a company/LLP)
  • Must be an Indian citizen — foreign nationals CANNOT form an OPC
  • Cannot be a minor
  • A person can be a member of ONLY 1 OPC at any time
  • An OPC CANNOT be converted into a Section 8 company
  • An OPC CANNOT carry on NBFI activities

Under Rule 4 (as amended in 2023):

  • Get WRITTEN CONSENT from the proposed nominee before incorporation
  • Mention the nominee's name in the e-MoA
  • File the nominee's consent as a DECLARATION within SPICe+

Change of nominee: Any time by the member — filed via Form INC-4 within 30 days of change.

Under Rule 6 (as amended in 2021): The old mandatory conversion thresholds were REMOVED. Now conversion is VOLUNTARY and can happen at any time:

  • Alter e-MoA and e-AoA by passing a resolution
  • Increase members to minimum 2 (Private) or 7 (Public)
  • Maintain minimum paid-up capital for that class
  • File e-Form INC-6 with altered e-MoA and e-AoA
  • ROC examines latest audited financials → issues new Certificate

Under Section 3A, if the NUMBER OF MEMBERS falls BELOW the legal minimum AND the company carries on business for MORE THAN 6 MONTHS in that state — the remaining members are PERSONALLY LIABLE for ALL debts:

  • Private Company: minimum is 2 members — if below 2 for 6+ months → personal liability
  • Public Company: minimum is 7 members — if below 7 for 6+ months → personal liability
Company Name Rules

Under Rule 8(1), the ROC considers two names as IDENTICAL and will REJECT the new name if the only differences are:

  • Suffix words: 'Private', 'Pvt', 'Limited', 'Ltd', 'LLP'
  • Plural form of any word (e.g., 'Solution' vs 'Solutions')
  • Spacing, punctuation marks, or type/case of letters
  • Joining/separating words (e.g., 'InfoTech' vs 'Info Tech')
  • Phonetic/spelling variations
  • Internet suffixes (.com, .net, .org, .in)
  • Prefixes: 'New', 'Modern', 'Nav', 'Shri', 'Sri', 'Om', 'Jai', 'Sai', 'The'

Under Rule 8B, the following words CANNOT be used without prior Central Government approval:

  • Insurance, Bank, Stock Exchange, Venture Capital
  • Asset Management, Nidhi, Mutual Fund
  • 'State' — allowed ONLY for Government companies
  • Name of any continent/country/state/city alone
  • Purely generic/descriptive names

A name is undesirable if:

  • It violates the Emblems and Names (Prevention of Improper Use) Act, 1950
  • It includes a registered trademark — UNLESS owner gives written consent
  • It is offensive to any section of people
  • It is identical to or too similar to an existing LLP name
  • It gives a misleading impression about scale/scope of business
  • It includes the name of a foreign country — without proof of MOU

Under Rule 9, you have two options:

Option 1 — RUN (Reserve Unique Name) — file separately first:

  • Apply via RUN web service on mca.gov.in
  • Propose up to 2 names with reasons
  • Pay the prescribed fee
  • If approved — name reserved for 20 days

Option 2 — SPICe+ Part A — name reservation + incorporation together:

  • If filing Part A separately — propose up to 2 names
  • If filing Parts A & B together — only 1 name required
Drafting MoA & AoA

Under Rule 10 / Section 5(3)–(5), 'Entrenchment provisions' are AoA clauses that make certain provisions HARDER to amend than the usual Special Resolution (75% vote) threshold.

  • Can be included in the ORIGINAL AoA at incorporation, OR added later
  • If added AFTER in a PRIVATE company — requires unanimous agreement of ALL members
  • For a PUBLIC company — requires a Special Resolution to add entrenchment
  • The company MUST notify the ROC of entrenchment provisions in SPICe+

Under Rule 13 / Rule 38, for all companies incorporated through SPICe+, ELECTRONIC MoA and AoA are mandatory:

  • e-MoA = Form INC-33 (filed online as part of SPICe+)
  • e-AoA = Form INC-34 (filed online as part of SPICe+)
  • Both must be DIGITALLY SIGNED by ALL subscribers using their DSCs
  • Physical/wet signatures are NOT accepted for SPICe+ filers
  • Subscribers' photographs are NOT required in SPICe+ forms
The Incorporation Process — SPICe+ & Timelines

SPICe+ (Simplified Proforma for Incorporating Company Electronically Plus — Form INC-32), introduced under Rule 38 in February 2020, is a two-part integrated form:

  • Part A: Company name reservation
  • Part B: Full incorporation filing with all documents

SPICe+ is India's Single-Window Company Registration System that allows you to apply for Company Incorporation, DSC, DIN, PAN, TAN, Telephone, Mobile, Email, and Bank Account Registration all in ONE form.

  1. Obtain DSC (Digital Signature Certificate) for each proposed director
  2. If directors don't have DIN — apply for new DINs within SPICe+
  3. Login to MCA21 portal (mca.gov.in)
  4. Open SPICe+ — file Part A for name (separately or with Part B)
  5. Fill Part B — company type, capital, registered office, director details
  6. Prepare e-MoA (INC-33) and e-AoA (INC-34) — digitally signed
  7. Fill AGILE-Pro (INC-35) — GSTIN, EPFO, ESIC, bank account details
  8. Attach all mandatory documents
  9. Generate challan and pay all applicable fees online
  10. Submit and respond to any ROC queries — up to 2 resubmissions allowed

Mandatory documents under Rules 12 & 16:

  • e-MoA (Form INC-33) — digitally signed by all subscribers
  • e-AoA (Form INC-34) — digitally signed by all subscribers
  • Declaration by CA/CS/CWA/Advocate in Form INC-8
  • INC-9 — Affidavit by each subscriber and first director
  • Registered office proof: Utility bill + ownership document OR notarised rent agreement
  • NOC from property owner if premises is rented
  • DIR-2 — Consent of each director
  • DIR-8 — Declaration that directors are not disqualified
  • PAN card copies of all subscribers/directors
  • Identity proof (Aadhaar / Passport / Voter ID)
  • Address proof of all subscribers/directors

Under Section 10A read with Rule 23A, every company WITH SHARE CAPITAL cannot start business or borrow money until:

  • ALL subscribers pay up the full value of their shares
  • A declaration in Form INC-20A is filed with the ROC
  • This MUST be done within 180 days of Certificate of Incorporation date

Consequences of non-compliance:

  • Company CANNOT start business operations
  • Company CANNOT borrow any money
  • ROC may initiate STRIKE OFF (close) the company
  • Penalty on company: ₹50,000
  • Penalty on each director: ₹1,000 per day (maximum ₹1,00,000)
Registered Office Setup & Requirements

If OWNED by the director/subscriber:

  • Utility bill (electricity/gas/telephone) NOT older than 2 months
  • PLUS: Ownership document (property tax receipt / title deed)

If RENTED:

  • Utility bill not older than 2 months
  • PLUS: Notarised Rent Agreement OR Lease Agreement
  • PLUS: NOC from property owner

If CO-WORKING SPACE / VIRTUAL OFFICE:

  • Agreement with co-working space operator
  • NOC from co-working space
  • Utility bill for that address

Under Rule 26, every company must PROMINENTLY display at its registered office:

  • Full company name
  • Address of the registered office
  • Corporate Identity Number (CIN)
  • Telephone number, email address, and website (if any)

On ALL business correspondence (letterheads, notices, bills, invoices, cheques, receipts, contracts):

  • Company name
  • Registered office address
  • CIN
  • Telephone, email, website (if any)
Common Mistakes Founders Must Avoid

✅ Correct Approach: Search BOTH mca.gov.in (company name) AND ipindia.gov.in (trademark) before finalising any name

✅ Correct Approach: Write broad objects to cover current business PLUS future areas (tech/AI/consulting/hardware etc.)

✅ Correct Approach: File INC-20A as soon as share capital is received in bank — don't wait for day 179

✅ Correct Approach: Only Indian citizens can form an OPC — NRIs qualify, but foreign nationals do NOT

✅ Correct Approach: Arrange address before incorporating — file INC-22 within 30 days of CoI date

⚖️ Legal Consequence: Criminal liability — Section 7(5)/(6) — imprisonment up to 10 years.

✅ Correct Approach: Always submit accurate documents

Helpful Tips for Smooth Incorporation

Start Early Begin the incorporation process at least 2-3 weeks before you plan to start operations. It gives you buffer time for document verification and ROC queries.
Verify Documents Double-check all documents for accuracy before uploading. A small mistake can delay your incorporation by weeks.
Use Authorized Service Providers If you're not familiar with the process, hire a qualified Company Secretary (CS) or CA. The investment is worth the saved time and hassle.
File INC-20A Immediately Don't wait until day 179. File the Commencement of Business form as soon as you receive share capital in the company's bank account.
Registered Office Setup Arrange your registered office address BEFORE filing SPICe+. This prevents delays in obtaining the Certificate of Incorporation.
Monitor ROC Portal Check the MCA21 portal regularly for any ROC queries or notices. Respond quickly to avoid rejection.
